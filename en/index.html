Offline Event Planning

Primary Role: Project Manager & Lead Planner

1. "Just Try It" Double 11 Campaign

To break the traditional definition of Double 11 as just a "Shopping Festival," we created the "Yi Xia (Try It) Festival" for young people. Centered on the core proposition of "Life has no limits, just try everything," the project adopted an O2O (Online-to-Offline) model. Offline, a flash mob experience was launched at the Shenzhen Software Industrial Base, featuring interactive segments like "Empty the Cart" and "Group Buy." Online, we initiated the "Try It Stories" topic collection, inviting users to share their own "try it" moments. Managed under a limited budget with multiple workstreams, the project covered creativity, design, communication, vendor coordination, and on-site execution. The event attracted over 300 offline participants, while the online topic reached over 10,000 exposures, successfully conveying the brand philosophy and receiving positive user feedback.

2. CIWEI Curiosity Brand Conference

The CIWEI Curiosity Brand Conference is the company's flagship annual brand project, aimed at deeply observing the values and career trends of Gen Z (born 2000-2005) and building a bridge for corporate HRs and Employer Brand leads to connect with future talent.

As the Lead Planner and Project Manager, I was responsible for the creative conception and execution, creating an immersive brand experience. In terms of guest invitation, I successfully invited over 60 HR representatives from top-tier enterprises such as P&G, Midea, and TCL, receiving enthusiastic feedback and unanimous praise. Through the release of the "Gen Z Curiosity Starry Sky Report," the hosting of the "Unconventional Youth" roundtable forum, and the presentation of several Youth-Friendly Employer Brand awards, the conference not only strengthened CIWEI's industry influence but also provided forward-looking insights for corporate talent strategies in the next decade.
